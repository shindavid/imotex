Let $f: \[0, 1\] \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ be a function such that

(i) $f(1) = 1,
(ii) $f(x) \geq 0$ for all $x \in \[0, 1\]$,
(iii) if $x, y$ and $x+y$ all lie in $\[0, 1\]$, then $f(x+y) \geq f(x) + f(y)$.

Prove that $f(x) \leq 2x$ for all $x \in \[0, 1\]$.
